Before First Period (Adaptation - Major): Grace Nelson

Interior: School hallway, morning. Amy (15) walks through the halls, her eyes alert. 

(Sound: Quiet buzzing from the lights, slow footsteps.) 

Amy: (under her breath to herself) Why does it feel so weird today? 

(Amy stops in front of a small group of students. She recognizes two of them as her friends Sara (14) and Al (15).) 

Amy: Hey. 

Sara: (turning to her) Hi. 

Al: (icily) Hello. 

Amy: Doesn’t the school seem a little too (pauses) hollow today? Everything feels so quiet and emotionless. 

Al: (curtly) No. 

Amy: It’s so quiet, though. 

Sara: It’s fine, Amy.  

Amy: But...

(Before she can finish her sentence, Sara walks away with Al and the other students.) 

(Amy groans and walks in the opposite direction, stopping when she sees her favorite teacher, Ms. Johanna (40s). Ms. Johanna is still and stiff with a blank expression on her face.) 

Amy: Hi, Ms. Johanna! I’m excited for your test today. 

Ms. Johanna: (monotone) Hello, Amy. I hope you have studied this week. 

Amy: Of course I have, Ms. Johanna. 

Ms. Johanna: (flat) Good luck, then. 

Amy: (confused) Are you okay? You’re usually so smiley. 

Ms. Johanna: I am fine, child. 

(Amy walks away quickly, clearly scared.) 

Amy: (to herself, voice quavering) Everything is fine. It’s all in your head, Amy. Focus on your usual routine. Go talk to Ms. Ellory. She always loves to chat in the morning. (Amy takes a few deep breaths, and her expression clears.) 

(Amy walks farther down the hall until she sees Ms. Ellory. Ms. Ellory looks eerily similar to Ms. Johanna.) 

Amy: Hi, Ms. Ellory! 

Ms. Ellory: (monotone) Hello, Amy. Shouldn’t you be getting to class? 

Amy: I still have ten minutes before first period.  

Ms. Ellory: (coolly) Shouldn’t you be getting to class? 

(Before Amy can respond, an announcement sounds from the speakers.) 

Principal Tim: (from the speakers, his voice calm but serious) All students are to report to the cafeteria immediately. Repeat. All students are to report to the cafeteria immediately. This is not a drill. Repeat. This is not a drill. 

(Sound: Complete silence.)

Amy: (terrified) What’s going on?

(A large group of wide-eyed students walks toward the cafeteria, guided by various teachers. The teachers look nearly identical, move in perfect unison, and stare into space blankly.) 

Amy: (louder, her voice practically a scream) What’s going on?
