Before First Period (Adaptation - Major): Miguel Rivero

(sounds, quiet, barely any sounds)

Narrator: Jack arrived at school earlier than usual. The building looked exactly the same as it always did, but the quiet felt heavier than normal.

Jack, (to self) “Dang, why is everything so… quiet”

Narrator, Jack passed a small group of other students and spoke to them.

Jack, do y’all know why everything is so quiet?

Students, (in a calm voice, at the same time) today is different

Jack, (to students) “why”

 Narrator, the other students walked away. Like if they did not want to say something.

Narrator: A few steps later, the student saw the first teacher of the day, coach Beckham. Expecting the usual greeting or routine behavior, Jack spoke first.

Jack (in a cheerful tone) “Hi coach Beckham!”

 Coach Beckham (in a robotic tone) “Hello Jack, have a nice day.”

Jack, (walking away), (in a confused tone) “That was odd”

Narrator, Jack continued walking and tried to calm themself by focusing on a familiar routine. For a moment, this helped. Everything still looked normal, and the other students seemed unconcerned.

Narrator, Then Jack encountered coach Beckham again, but farther down the hall, which was impossible. Because he was farther down the hall in the opposite direction.

Jack, (confused) “how...how did he get here?”

 Jack (to Coach Beckham) how did you get there without walking past me?

 Coach Beckham, “Hello Jack, have a nice day”.

Narrator, now, Jack knew something was wrong.

As Jack reacted, a school announcement interrupted. The voice over the loudspeaker sounded calm but serious and said instruction meant to keep them safe.

Principal’s loudspeaker: “students, all the teachers today are not feeling well, if you see a teacher, don’t look at their eyes.”

Narrator, Jack spoke again, realizing that this was not going to end well.

Jack, “whatever this is, I’m cooked!”

Narrator, What Jack did not understand was that the teachers were no longer acting as individuals, while the other students were already being guided away.
