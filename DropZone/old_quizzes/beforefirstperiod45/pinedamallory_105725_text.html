Before First Period (Adaptation - Major): Mallory Pineda

Interior: School hallway leading to classrooms. Mason (15) walking down the hallway to his first class of the day. A group of teenagers talk on the side of the hallway.

(Sound: Quiet, rushed, whispering from the group) their gaze followed Mason as soon as he turned his back.

(Mason notices the tones of words are sharper and louder than usual)

(Mason passed by one of his teachers in the hallway, Mrs. Williams (40s), she was standing outside her door with a blank stare on her face)

Mason: (smiling) Good Morning, Mrs. Williams.

(He expected the same warm excited tone as usual, but she sounded different. Stranger than normal, almost a robotic switch was controlling her)

Mrs. Williams: (low, monotone) Good Morning, Mason.

(Mason felt uneased at the sound of it)

Sound: shoes squeaking to class, low talking in background)

(Mason passes by another teacher, Mr. Rick (mid 20s), he looks the same as Mrs. Williams, standing outside his door with a blank stare on his face.)

Mason: (trying to forget what just happened) Good morning, Mr. Rick. Feeling good or feeling amazing!

(Mr. Rick didn’t even look down to acknowledge him, he continued to stare blankly)

Mr. Rick: (low, monotone, voice) Good morning, Mason.

(He sounds the exact same way as Mrs. Williams, too)

(Mason is sure something is wrong; he nearly gets a word out when he is interrupted)

Mason: (confused) What is going on tod---.

(sound: screechy announcement static, kids stop to listen)

(Announcements start, Mr. Chide (late 50s) is speaking)

Mr. Chide: (Low hurried voice) Remember dolphins, always have some form of protection on you, whether it’s scissors, a pencil, or a Stanley stay safe in case of any dangerous activity in the school. And remember if you see something, say something. Stay safe children. (Ending static begins)

(Sound: Static from the speakers ends, the hallway is silent, students stop and look at each other, not in confusion but in understanding and determination.)

(Mason is confused and a little scared. He tries to look for a teacher to talk to about this weird announcement, but everyone is either gone or rushed into classrooms by an administrator.)

Mason: (Shakey, scared, voice) Somethings wrong. What’s going on here!?

(Mason now realizes that the teachers are no longer their own individuals, but something else, they are one.)
